Sponsors

Africa50 is an infrastructure investment platform jointly established by a range of African governments and the African Development Bank. With US$881m in committed capital, the organization has a mandate to help bridge Africa’s infrastructure funding gap by facilitating project development, mobilizing public and private sector finance, and investing in infrastructure on the continent across several sectors including power, transport, midstream gas, ICT, healthcare, education and fintech

Visit website

The Rwanda Development Board (“RDB”) is a Rwandan government institution mandated to accelerate Rwanda’s economic development by enabling private sector growth; and serves as the shareholder representative of the Rwandan Government within KICCL. The RDB was established in 2008 out of a merger of eight government institutions, with its primary function being to drive Rwandan economic growth by serving the private sector as both the interface for the Rwandan government and as a business enabler

Board of Directors

Tshepidi Moremong

Chief Operating Officer at Africa50 with more than 22 years of experience delivering strategic leadership in private equity, corporate finance and business development. Prior to joining Africa50, Tshepidi was the Head of Client Coverage for the rest of Africa outside South Africa, a role she has held since 2014 and many other leadership roles in financial services industry. Ms. Moremong holds a Bachelor’s degree in Economics and Public Policy from Swarthmore College in Pennsylvania (USA) and an MBA in Finance from Columbia University’s Business School in New York (USA)

Tesi Rusagara

Oversees RDB’s innovation portfolio including a venture capital fund and Kigali Innovation City. She was previously with Deloitte Consulting in San Francisco advising on digital transformation, growth strategy and divestitures in financial services. She started her career in the President of Rwanda’s Strategy and Policy Unit and in Bank of Kigali. She serves as a Board Member of the Rwanda Capital Markets Authority. She holds a Bachelor of Business Science (Finance) from the University of Cape Town (SA) and an MBA from Stanford University (USA)

Kaniaru Wacieni

A Senior Investment Director at Africa50 with over 17 years of financial experience. Prior to joining Africa50 in October 2019, he was a Business Development Director at Globeleq LP for 3 years. Before this, Kaniaru was an Executive Director at J.P. Morgan’s Power & Utilities group in New York, where he advised on M&A, leveraged buyouts, restructurings, and debt and equity financings. He began his career in 1999 at Credit Suisse First Boston in New York and London in the Industrials and TMT investment banking sectors. He holds a BA from Harvard University (honors) and an MBA from Harvard
